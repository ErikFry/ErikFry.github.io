Leeds United

Injury update!

Injuries and absentees cost Leeds United greatly on their return to action following the international break. Marcelo Bielsa's side went into the two-week layoff on the back of their first Premier League win of the campaign, after Watford were dispatched at Elland Road last time out. But any hope that they would be able to carry any momentum forward was dashed following a disappointing 1-0 defeat to Southampton at St Mary's. Marcelo Bielsa was forced into three changes for the south coast trip, with Junior Firpo, Kalvin Philllips and Raphinha all dropping out of the starting XI. Read more..

Calvin Philllips

Although Kalvin Phillips travelled to Southampton, he was not named in Marcelo Bielsa's matchday squad. A calf strain saw him withdraw from Gareth Southgate's injury squad, with Bielsa confirming after the Saints defeat that he has also been carrying a hip problem. "In the case of Phillips, in the two weeks after the Watford game, he was working with an injury that was bothering him, that was a muscular problem in his calf," said the Whites boss following the 1-0 defeat. "During those days, for a while now he's had a problem in his hip and those two joined together, and even if he was close to being able to play the game, the conditions weren't the necessary ones for him to play." Expect another update when Bielsa faces the press this week ahead of Wolves.
